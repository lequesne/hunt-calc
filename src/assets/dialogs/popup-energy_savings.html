Annual Energy Savings

To calculate the ongoing savings on energy bills, our point of comparison is the traditional set up people have in their homes. This is a home with gas hot water, ducted air conditioning, no solar panels and a conventional vehicle.

Energy usage charges

Huntlee Plus's energy usage charges (tariffs) are designed to provide benefits, no matter what energy choices you make when living in the community.

The starting point for all Huntlee residents' energy prices is a base discount of 2% in comparison to Energy Australia's Standing Offer (ENE130070SR, 4/02/2016).

Vehicle assumptions

Fuel cost savings were calculated using an average 13,800kms driven per year (ABS - 9208.0 - Survey of Motor Vehicle Use, Australia, 12 months ended 31 October 2014), a fuel price of $1.20/litre and an average fuel consumption of 9litres/100km. Electric Vehicle consumption was assumed to be 20kWh/100km (Average of Tesla S and Nissan Leaf).

Gas assumptions

Gas consumption was assumed to be:

  • 2BDR Townhouse– 9GJ per annum

  • 2BDR House– 9GJ per annum

  • 3BDR House– 13GJ per annum

  • 4BDR House– 13GJ per annum

  • 5BDR House– 14GJ per annum

Gas price assumed to be $41/GJ with a daily supply charge of $0.68.

Carbon Savings

We calculated the carbon savings using the Department of the Environment's National Greenhouse Accounts Factors.

To work out the CO2 comparison to fly around the equator we used the following numbers:

  • CO2 Emissions from long haul airplane = 0.23kg/km (http://www.carbonplanet.com/downloads/Flight_Calculator_Information_v9.2.pdf)
  • Seats on a Boeing 747-400 = 416 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boeing_747
  • Average occupancy on a flight assumed = 80%
  • Distance around equator = 40,075km http://education.nationalgeographic.org/encyclopedia/equator/
